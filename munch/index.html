project overview

Designing a meal-kit delivery app for busy health-conscious users

Munch is a meal kit delivery application that focuses on aiding busy individuals with health and fitness goals. This was a project I worked on from October 2023 to January 2024 for ACM at UC San Diego's Winter Project.

When: October 2023 - January 2024 (13 weeks)

Where: ACM at UC San Diego

Role: Design Lead + Researcher in team of 3

problem statement

The challenge of maintaining healthy eating habits in a busy world

I noticed that many people around me, including myself, struggle with eating balanced meals regularly. This became the foundation for our research into understanding the barriers to healthy eating.

research methods

Discovering what really matters to users through research

To examine the problem further, my team and I conducted a series of interviews and surveys, asking questions such as "What is your current method of trying to eat healthy?" and "Are there any challenges you encounter when it comes to meal prepping?"

Research Methods: 6 interviews, 34 surveys

Target Age: 20-32 years old

key findings

Key insights that shaped our design direction

Pain Point 1: Many people don't have time to cook everyday

Pain Point 2: Health and fitness-focused individuals struggle maintaining balanced diets

Pain Point 3: Meal prepping leads to repetitive meals and lack of variety

These findings revealed that busy individuals, especially college students and young professionals, face significant barriers to maintaining healthy eating habits due to time constraints and lack of variety in meal planning.

user persona

Bringing our users to life through persona development

With the insights gained from research and interviews, I created a persona to help represent our target users. Creating a persona solidified the pain points and helped identify the goals I wanted to accomplish through our design.

User persona representing our target audience

By analyzing the research we conducted, we came up with the idea of a meal kit delivery application that targets health-focused individuals because it can address all of the pain points that we discovered.

current solutions

Learning from the competition to find our opportunity

I conducted a competitive analysis to compare current solutions for meal delivery services and identify opportunities for improvement.

Standard Services: HelloFresh, Blue Apron, Home Chef

Health-Focused: Territory (diet meal kits)

Analysis of existing meal delivery services

design gaps

Identifying gaps in the current market

After examining the competition, my team and I identified key design gaps in existing solutions:

Limited Customization: Users can input general dietary preferences but can't fully customize each meal to their liking

Lack of User Control: Missing back buttons and difficult navigation flows

Poor User Experience: Limited freedom when creating meal kits

user flow design

From ideas to user flows through collaborative ideation

Now that the design gaps were identified, I worked with my team to brainstorm possible user flows. We used FigJam to brainstorm a logical user flow for ordering a new meal kit, doing this multiple times for each flow in our app.

Process: Brainstormed possible user flows

Iterations: Multiple iterations of each screen

Final Selection: Combined favorite ideas into organized low-fidelity user flow sketch

User flow brainstorming and sketching process

mid-fidelity prototype

Bringing our ideas to life with interactive prototypes

With the rough sketches, I transferred them into Figma wireframes and connected the frames together to create a functional prototype ready for usability testing.

Transfer: Moved lo-fi sketches into Figma

Functionality: Turned static wireframes into functional prototype

Focus: "Build a meal kit" flow (primary focus)

Mid-fidelity wireframes ready for testing

usability testing

Putting our designs to the test with real users

Within the time constraints of this project, we conducted two rounds of usability testing with 12 participants aged 20-32.

Round 1: Mid-fidelity prototype with basic tasks

Round 2: High-fidelity prototype with complex tasks

Tasks: "Start a new meal kit order" and "Add this recipe to your favorites"

Usability testing sessions with participants

improvement 1: review order page

Making meal selection clearer and more intuitive

Problem: 4/6 users expressed confusion regarding the number of meals per week selection. Users selected this at the beginning but the number didn't appear later in the flow.

Solution: I used Figma's variables feature to capture the user's selection and display it throughout the flow, showing how many servings are in the cart and how many more are needed.

Impact: This change offers more support for users when adjusting servings because they don't have to remember their initial selection.

Updated review order page with meal count display

improvement 2: nutrition summary

Giving users complete nutritional transparency

Problem: 3/6 participants mentioned that remembering nutrition facts for every recipe was tedious. Previously, users had to click into each recipe card to see nutrition information.

Solution: I added a "Nutrition Summary" tab in the review page that shows the average macronutrient and calorie breakdown across all selected meals.

Impact: Users can now see the overall nutritional balance of their entire meal kit at a glance.

Nutrition summary tab showing macro breakdown

improvement 3: macro toggle

Putting nutrition information front and center

Problem: Users had to take an extra step to view nutritional information, and some might not open cards at all.

Solution: I created a new card design that displays macros in the card preview, with a toggle option for users who don't want to see calorie information.

Impact: Users can choose meals based on health needs at a glance, with control over their experience.

Recipe cards with macro preview and toggle option

complete user flow

A seamless meal kit creation journey

After implementing all improvements, the final design includes a complete meal kit building flow with enhanced user experience and nutritional transparency.

Meal Selection: Pick meals with macro previews displayed

Customization: Swap out ingredients users don't want

Nutrition Summary: Holistic breakdown of entire meal kit

Checkout: Streamlined final review and purchase

Complete "Build a Meal Kit" user flow

visual design system

Creating a cohesive visual language for health and wellness

I compiled a comprehensive style guide containing typography, color palette, and interactive components. The design system was adapted from my personal design library, customized for the health-focused nature of the app.

Color Research: Green represents nature and health

Design Library: Adapted from personal work-in-progress design system

Customization: Modified color styles, border radius, shadows for app-specific needs

Visual design system and component library

learning outcomes

Key learnings from building a product from scratch

Collaborative Design: Working through end-to-end design process collaboratively requires significant goal alignment

Time Management: Should be heavily prioritized when there's a time constraint for group projects

Figma Variables: Learning to use Figma's variables feature made prototypes much more functional and realistic

This project was my first time working with a group from the very first step all the way through the design process. Starting a design from scratch required spending considerable time aligning our goals, but ultimately helped me learn that time management should be super high on the priority list for group projects, especially with strict time constraints.
